About Me

I was born in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. I moved to the United States when I was 2 years old. My family lived in New Jersey, then Philadelphia, and then Milwaukee. I grew up in Glendale. I went to the University of Wisconsin-Madison and graduated with a Bachelor of Science with Honors in Molecular Biology.


I attended the University of Wisconsin Medical School, but I didn't graduate. I ended up getting a job with a law firm in Milwaukee as a marketing assistant. This grew into a wonderful career running a nonprofit legal trade association: The National Association of Minority and Women Law Firms.


I attended Marquette University Law School and graduated in 2010. I initially worked for the same law firm, but eventually started my own business.


During my lifetime, I have traveled to Belize and India: Belize in 2003 and India in 2008 (and 2015). I have also traveled around the United States, to California, Louisiana, New York, Pennsylvania, Ohio, Illinois, Minnesota, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, and Texas.
